This paper develops a non-survey-based strategy to estimate a metropolitan-scale interregional input-output coefficient matrix, using Greater Santa Fe as a case study. The methodological approach consists of “regionalizing” the national technical coefficient matrix. The data sources include Argentina’s 2018 input-output table estimated by the OECD and employment data collected by INDEC. Based on the estimation, the productive structure of the GSF is characterized in terms of productive linkages and multiplier effects. The analysis reconstructs the economic landscape of the region, identifying its strategic, driving, key, and independent sectors.
