This article examines the recent rise and subsequent stagnation of productivity in Argentina through a subsystems approach. Specifically, indicators based on hyper vertically integrated (HVI) sectors are developed using OECD Input-Output Tables. The analysis spans the period 2004–2019, harmonizing activities across 36 sectors. The results confirm that productivity growth during 2004–2011 was driven by improved macroeconomic conditions. From 2010 onwards, a declining trend in productivity is observed, highlighting a concentrated pattern of technical change and evidence of disinvestment in both direct and indirect labour, as well as, to a lesser extent, changes in demand composition associated with the increasing importance of services.
